Vaughan’s Biting Pest Hotspots

Vaughan sits at the northern edge of the GTA where suburban development meets agricultural land and protected greenspace. This urban-rural interface is the defining factor in Vaughan’s biting pest landscape. Communities like Maple and Kleinburg border active farmland and the Kortright Centre conservation area, creating ideal conditions for ticks in tall grass and wooded borders. Properties near conservation land face elevated tick risk from May through October, particularly for families with children and pets.

Woodbridge and Concord, Vaughan’s more established communities along the Humber River valley, face strong biting pest pressure. Carpenter ants thrive in the mature-treed streets, targeting moisture-damaged wood along rooflines and decks. Spiders migrate indoors seasonally from the ravine corridors, and bed bugs are an increasing concern in the growing condo and townhome stock along the Highway 7 corridor.

Wasps are a major summer concern across all of Vaughan. The city’s predominantly suburban character — large lots, vinyl-clad exteriors, landscaped gardens, and backyard structures — provides abundant nesting sites for paper wasps, yellow jackets, and hornets. Fleas are also common in pet-friendly homes throughout Vaughan. Our Vaughan technicians combine tick treatment, wasp removal, ant control, and bed bug expertise to address the complete range of biting pests generated by this unique urban-rural setting.
