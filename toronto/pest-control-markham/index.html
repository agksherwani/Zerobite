Markham’s Biting Pest Hotspots

Markham is a city of contrasts — heritage conservation areas in Unionville and Markham Village sit alongside some of York Region’s newest master-planned communities in Cornell, Berczy, and Cathedraltown. This mix of old and new housing produces a wide range of pest challenges. In Unionville’s historic core, century-old homes with original wooden frames, fieldstone foundations, and mature trees are prime targets for carpenter ants. Large hardwoods overhanging rooflines create moisture bridges that carpenter ants follow directly into fascia, soffits, and structural members.

Markham Village faces similar issues, with its tree-canopied streets and character homes providing ideal conditions for both carpenter ants and wasps. Wasps build nests under the ornate eaves and trim of older homes, while spiders thrive in basements and crawl spaces. These heritage areas see consistent biting pest activity from spring through fall.

In contrast, Markham’s newer communities face ticks from remaining field edges and wasps nesting on large suburban lots. Cornell, Berczy, and Cathedraltown border conservation lands where ticks are prevalent in tall grass and wooded borders. Bed bugs are a growing concern in Markham’s expanding condo stock along Highway 7. Our Markham technicians are equally comfortable treating carpenter ant infestations in 150-year-old Unionville homes and providing tick treatment for properties near conservation areas.
