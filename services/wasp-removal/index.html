Professional Wasp & Hornet Removal Across Ontario

Ontario is home to several species of stinging wasps and hornets, and encounters with these insects are an inevitable part of the warmer months. While a single wasp at a backyard barbecue is a nuisance, a nest attached to your home or hidden in a wall cavity is a genuine safety hazard. Wasps and hornets are territorial and will aggressively defend their nests, delivering multiple painful stings to anyone perceived as a threat. For the approximately three percent of Canadians who are allergic to insect venom, even a single sting can trigger anaphylaxis, a life-threatening reaction that requires immediate medical intervention.

At ZeroBite Pest Control, we treat wasp and hornet nest removal as an urgent safety issue. Our licensed technicians respond same-day whenever possible, arrive with full protective equipment, and use targeted application methods that eliminate the colony while minimizing risk to your family, pets, and the environment. We do not simply knock down nests and hope for the best. Our approach involves treating the colony at its source, removing the physical nest, and applying preventive treatments to discourage wasps from rebuilding in the same location.

Common Wasp and Hornet Species in Ontario

Understanding which species you are dealing with is essential for safe and effective removal. Ontario's most common stinging insects include yellow jackets, paper wasps, bald-faced hornets, and European hornets, each with distinct nesting habits and aggression levels.

Yellow jackets are among the most aggressive species in Ontario. They build nests underground in abandoned rodent burrows, inside wall voids, and in other enclosed spaces. Their nests can contain several thousand workers by late summer, and they are notoriously defensive, capable of stinging multiple times. Yellow jackets are attracted to sugary foods and proteins, making them a persistent problem at outdoor dining areas, patios, and near garbage receptacles.

Paper wasps build the distinctive open-celled, umbrella-shaped nests commonly found under eaves, porch ceilings, and deck overhangs. Their colonies are smaller than yellow jackets, typically numbering a few dozen to a few hundred workers. While less aggressive than yellow jackets, paper wasps will sting if their nest is disturbed, and their nests are often built in high-traffic areas where accidental encounters are likely.

Bald-faced hornets construct large, enclosed grey nests in trees, shrubs, and on building exteriors. These nests can grow to the size of a basketball or larger and contain several hundred workers. Bald-faced hornets are extremely aggressive when their nest is threatened and can mobilize the entire colony to attack. Their stings are particularly painful, and they can sting repeatedly. Attempting to remove a bald-faced hornet nest without professional equipment is extremely dangerous.

Why DIY Wasp Removal Is Dangerous

Store-bought wasp sprays and home remedies may seem like cost-effective solutions, but they frequently make the situation worse and put you at serious risk. Spraying a nest during the day when the colony is active can trigger a mass attack. Over-the-counter aerosol products rarely penetrate deep enough into wall voids or underground nests to reach the queen, meaning the colony survives and rebuilds. Attempting to seal nest entrances traps wasps inside wall cavities, forcing them to chew their way into your living space.

ZeroBite technicians are equipped with bee suits, professional-grade application equipment, and the training to handle aggressive species safely. We treat nests during optimal times, apply products that reach the queen and all workers, and physically remove the nest structure to prevent re-colonization. For nests in wall voids, we carefully open the cavity, remove the nest, and seal the entry point to prevent future nesting.

Nest Prevention Strategies

Removing a wasp nest solves the immediate problem, but prevention is equally important. Wasps are creatures of habit, and they tend to build nests in the same locations year after year. ZeroBite applies residual treatments to common nesting sites including eaves, soffits, deck undersides, and fence posts. These products deter queens from starting new nests in treated areas during the spring nest-building period.

We also recommend practical steps homeowners can take to reduce wasp attraction: keeping garbage bins sealed, cleaning up fallen fruit from fruit trees, sealing gaps and cracks in your home's exterior, installing screens on soffit vents, and avoiding leaving sugary drinks and food outdoors uncovered.

When to Call for Wasp Removal

The best time to address a wasp nest is as early as possible. In spring, when queens first begin building, nests are small and colonies are manageable. By mid-summer, nests can contain hundreds or thousands of workers and removal becomes significantly more complex and dangerous. If you notice wasp activity around your property in spring or early summer, call ZeroBite for an inspection before the colony grows. For nests discovered later in the season, our technicians are equipped to handle even large, established colonies safely.

Emergency calls for wasp removal are common during August and September when colonies are at peak population and food sources begin to dwindle. Wasps become increasingly aggressive during this period, actively competing for food at outdoor events, near garbage collection areas, and around restaurant patios. ZeroBite provides same-day emergency response for dangerous nest situations throughout Ontario.
