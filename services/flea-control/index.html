Professional Flea Extermination Across Ontario

Ontario is home to millions of pets, and with them comes one of the most persistent household pests: fleas. Whether your dog picks them up at the park, your cat encounters them through contact with wildlife, or they hitch a ride indoors on your clothing, fleas can establish a thriving population inside your home remarkably fast. Ontario’s warm indoor environments allow fleas to breed year-round, meaning an infestation that starts in summer can persist straight through winter without professional intervention.

ZeroBite Pest Control provides comprehensive flea elimination for Ontario homeowners that goes far beyond surface-level treatment. Our dual-action approach combines an adulticide that kills fleas on contact with an insect growth regulator (IGR) that prevents eggs and larvae from developing into biting adults. This combination breaks the flea lifecycle at multiple stages, ensuring the infestation is truly eliminated rather than temporarily suppressed. We treat every floor surface, upholstered piece of furniture, pet resting area, and the crevices where flea pupae hide.

Why Fleas Are So Difficult to Eliminate Without Professional Help

The flea lifecycle is the reason why over-the-counter sprays and flea bombs rarely solve the problem. Adult fleas represent only about five percent of the total flea population in your home. The remaining ninety-five percent exists as eggs, larvae, and pupae embedded deep in carpet fibres, upholstery, and floor cracks. Flea pupae are encased in sticky, silk cocoons that are virtually impervious to chemical treatment and can remain dormant for months, only emerging when they detect vibration, warmth, or carbon dioxide from a nearby host.

This lifecycle explains the seemingly endless cycle of reinfestation that frustrates Ontario pet owners who have tried DIY treatments. ZeroBite’s IGR-based treatment prevents the next generation from ever reaching adulthood, while the residual adulticide kills newly emerged adults for weeks after application. Combined with proper pet treatment from your veterinarian, our approach achieves permanent elimination within two to four weeks.

Protecting Both Pets and Humans

Fleas are not just an annoyance — they pose real health risks to both pets and people. Flea bites cause intense itching that leads to secondary skin infections from scratching. Many pets develop flea allergy dermatitis (FAD), an allergic reaction to flea saliva that causes severe skin inflammation, hair loss, and hot spots. In heavy infestations, puppies and kittens can develop anaemia from blood loss. Fleas can also transmit tapeworm to both pets and humans, and are known carriers of murine typhus and bartonellosis (cat scratch disease).

For families with young children who play on floors and carpets, flea infestations are particularly concerning. ZeroBite’s pet-safe treatment products are Health Canada-registered and safe for children and pets once dry, typically within two to four hours of application. We provide detailed preparation and re-entry instructions to ensure the safety of every household member.

Flea Reproduction Fact

A single female flea lays up to 50 eggs per day. Those eggs fall off your pet and into your carpet, furniture, and bedding. Under favourable indoor conditions, the entire lifecycle from egg to biting adult takes just two to three weeks. This means a small initial introduction can produce thousands of fleas within a month. Professional treatment with IGR technology is the only reliable way to break this explosive breeding cycle.

What to Expect During Flea Treatment

Your flea treatment begins with a free inspection to assess the severity of the infestation and identify all affected areas. Before treatment day, you will need to vacuum all floors and upholstered furniture thoroughly, wash all pet bedding on hot, and clear floors of clutter for maximum treatment access. All people and pets must vacate during treatment and for two to four hours afterward while products dry.

Our technician applies the adulticide-IGR combination to all floor surfaces, under furniture, along baseboards, and on upholstered furniture. You may see increased flea activity for one to two weeks after treatment as dormant pupae emerge and encounter the residual product — this is normal and expected. A follow-up visit at two weeks addresses any remaining activity. Full elimination typically takes two to four weeks from the initial treatment.
