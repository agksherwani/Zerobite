Stratford's Biting Pest Hotspots

Stratford is defined by two things that directly impact its pest landscape: stunning Victorian architecture and a world-renowned theatre festival that draws hundreds of thousands of visitors annually. The festival season runs from April through November, filling Stratford’s hotels, bed-and-breakfasts, and vacation rentals with guests from across the globe. This high-volume, high-turnover hospitality environment makes bed bugs Stratford’s most commercially damaging pest — a single infestation can result in cancelled bookings, negative reviews, and thousands in lost revenue.

Erie Street and the downtown core are home to some of Ontario’s finest restaurants and shops, and the outdoor dining patios and garden spaces attract wasps throughout summer. Our targeted wasp and hornet removal keeps Stratford’s hospitality businesses safe for guests and staff, with rapid response times during peak festival season.

Stratford’s residential neighbourhoods — from the grand Victorian homes near the Avon River to the East End and West End — face their own biting pest challenges. Century-old brick construction with ornamental woodwork and aging mortar provides carpenter ants with abundant entry points. Spiders thrive in the basements and crawl spaces of older homes, while ticks are a growing concern near the Avon River trails and surrounding parkland.
